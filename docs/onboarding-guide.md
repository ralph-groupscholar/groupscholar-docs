# Onboarding Guide

## Summary

This guide helps new teammates ramp into Group Scholar quickly, align on how we work,
and deliver their first meaningful contribution within two weeks.

## Day 1 essentials

- Confirm your manager, buddy, and first-week goals.
- Review the company overview and product brief.
- Read the operations playbook to understand weekly rituals.
- Get your device, accounts, and security settings squared away.
- Book your first 1:1s and team introductions.

## Week 1 priorities

- Map the org: who owns product, partnerships, and delivery.
- Shadow one program or customer workflow end-to-end.
- Join the weekly all-hands and capture open questions.
- Identify a quick win you can ship by the end of week two.

## Week 2 deliverables

- Share a short onboarding recap with your manager.
- Ship one meaningful contribution (doc update, workflow fix, or feature).
- Add yourself to the team roster and ownership matrix.

## Access checklist

- Email, calendar, and chat accounts active.
- Project tracker and document access verified.
- Cloud environments, dashboards, and data tools provisioned.
- Security: password manager, MFA, and device encryption enabled.

## Rituals

- Weekly all-hands: progress, blockers, and priorities.
- Team syncs: current work, dependencies, and help needed.
- 1:1s: feedback, growth goals, and context sharing.

## What good looks like

- You can explain Group Scholar's mission, programs, and customers.
- You know the roadmap for the current quarter.
- You understand how decisions are documented and communicated.
- You can identify the next two projects you will own.

## Where to get help

- Your buddy for day-to-day questions.
- Your manager for priorities and success metrics.
- Operations for access, tooling, and rituals.
- Engineering for platform or data questions.
