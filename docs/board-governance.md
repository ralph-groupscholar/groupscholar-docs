# Board & Governance Guide

## Summary

This guide outlines how Group Scholar partners with its board and advisors to ensure strategic alignment, accountability, and strong stewardship.

## Scope

- Applies to the board of directors, advisory boards, and executive leadership.
- Covers governance cadence, decision rights, and board materials.
- Excludes day-to-day operations (see `operations-playbook.md`).

## Governance principles

- **Mission stewardship**: protect scholar outcomes and equity commitments.
- **Strategic clarity**: focus board time on priorities and tradeoffs.
- **Operational trust**: leadership owns execution; board provides oversight.
- **Transparency**: clear metrics, risks, and decisions are visible.
- **Preparedness**: materials arrive early and are decision-ready.

## Board roles and responsibilities

- Approve strategic plans, annual budgets, and major program expansions.
- Provide risk oversight and ensure compliance readiness.
- Support fundraising through introductions and stewardship.
- Evaluate executive performance and succession readiness.
- Safeguard mission, values, and organizational health.

## Decision rights

- **Board approvals**: annual budget, strategic plan, executive compensation, major partnerships, and legal structure changes.
- **Executive decisions**: operating plans, staffing, program execution, and vendor selection.
- **Escalations**: mission risk, brand risk, or financial risk triggers board review.

## Board cadence

- Quarterly board meetings with a 90-minute agenda.
- Monthly metrics brief circulated asynchronously.
- Annual strategy retreat and budget approval.
- Ad hoc meetings for major risks or partnership decisions.

## Standard board materials

- Executive summary (1 page).
- KPI dashboard (impact, operational health, financials).
- Program status updates with highlights and risks.
- Fundraising pipeline and partner updates.
- Decision memo(s) with options and recommendation.

## Core metrics to track

- Scholar reach, award rates, and completion outcomes.
- Program quality indicators and satisfaction.
- Financial health (runway, revenue mix, burn, variance).
- Partnerships performance and renewal signals.
- Organizational health (hiring plan, retention, capacity).

## Committee structure

- **Audit & risk**: compliance, controls, and data privacy oversight.
- **Finance**: budget review and financial forecasting.
- **Program impact**: outcomes, evaluation, and research priorities.
- **Fundraising**: donor pipeline and sponsor stewardship.

## Board member onboarding

- Mission and origin story brief.
- Program portfolio overview and impact metrics.
- Current strategic plan and operating model.
- Governance calendar and key policies.
- Communication norms and escalation paths.

## Annual governance calendar

- Q1: approve annual plan, confirm KPIs, refresh risk register.
- Q2: program portfolio review and partnership expansion plan.
- Q3: fundraising review and budget forecast update.
- Q4: budget approval, executive review, and strategy planning.

## Board effectiveness check

- Annual self-assessment survey with action items.
- Track meeting attendance and contribution balance.
- Review decision cycle time and clarity of outcomes.
- Adjust committee scope or cadence as needed.

## Open questions

- Do we need a formal advisory council for scholar alumni?
- Which metrics should move to a quarterly deep dive?
