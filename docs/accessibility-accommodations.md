# Accessibility & Accommodations Guide

## Summary

This guide defines how Group Scholar plans accessible experiences and delivers timely accommodations for scholars, mentors, partners, and staff.

## Scope

- Digital experiences (applications, portals, email campaigns, PDFs).
- Live experiences (events, interviews, office hours, community sessions).
- Ongoing support (case management, communications, learning resources).

## Accessibility standards

- Use plain language, short paragraphs, and descriptive headings.
- Provide alt text for images and meaningful link text.
- Offer multiple formats for key assets (HTML + tagged PDF + text summary).
- Avoid color-only meaning and ensure sufficient contrast.
- Provide captions or transcripts for video and audio assets.

## Accommodation request workflow

- Intake channels: form, email alias, and support case intake.
- Response target: acknowledge within 1 business day, confirm plan within 3 business days.
- Intake fields: requestor, need, deadline, context, preferred format.
- Assign an owner and document the plan in the case record.
- Close the loop with confirmation and a feedback prompt.

## Event accessibility checklist

- Venue: step-free access, accessible restrooms, quiet space, clear signage.
- Program: breaks every 60-90 minutes, clear agenda, early access to materials.
- Tech: microphones, captioning vendor, accessible slide templates.

## Digital accessibility checklist

- Run a quick scan before publishing high-traffic pages.
- Validate PDFs for tags and reading order.
- Test key flows with keyboard-only navigation.

## Roles & responsibilities

- Program lead: approves accommodation plan and budget.
- Support lead: coordinates logistics and communicates updates.
- Content owner: ships accessible materials on schedule.

## Documentation & privacy

- Store accommodation notes in the support case record only.
- Limit access to staff with a direct need to know.
- Retain only what is required for delivery and legal compliance.

## Metrics to track

- Accommodation request volume by type.
- On-time fulfillment rate.
- Satisfaction rating from follow-up surveys.
- Repeat accommodation needs by program.

## Escalation

- Escalate if a request cannot be met within 3 business days.
- Flag legal or safety concerns to the safeguarding lead immediately.
