# Research & Insights Guide

## Summary
This guide defines how Group Scholar captures scholar, partner, and program insights to guide product and program decisions.

## Research goals
- Surface scholar needs, barriers, and success drivers.
- Validate program design choices with evidence.
- Reduce risk before launching new offerings.
- Keep partner feedback visible and actionable.

## Research intake
- Submit a short brief with the decision to be made and timeline.
- Name the audience, cohort, or partner segment to study.
- Confirm incentives, consent, and data privacy requirements.
- Assign a primary researcher and a decision owner.

## Standard methods
- Scholar interviews and listening sessions.
- Partner discovery calls and renewal retrospectives.
- Program retrospectives after each cohort.
- Survey pulses with a defined hypothesis.
- Field observations during live sessions.

## Sample size guidance
- 5-8 interviews for directional discovery.
- 10-15 interviews for high-stakes decisions.
- Surveys: aim for 30% response rate per cohort.

## Insight artifacts
- Research plan with questions and success criteria.
- Interview notes stored in a shared folder.
- Insight summary with top findings and implications.
- Decision log entry linked to the research summary.

## Feedback capture
- Capture feedback in a consistent template within 48 hours.
- Tag insights by program, cohort, and theme.
- Highlight urgent risks in the weekly ops update.

## Insight sharing cadence
- Weekly: short research highlights in ops update.
- Monthly: insight review for product and program leads.
- Quarterly: synthesis report with trends and gaps.

## Ethics and consent
- Always explain how data will be used.
- Offer opt-out paths and alternative ways to contribute.
- Avoid collecting sensitive data unless necessary.

## Escalation
- Escalate safety or wellbeing concerns immediately.
- Flag findings that impact partner commitments within 24 hours.
- Revisit research plan if goals or scope shift.
