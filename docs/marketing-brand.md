# Marketing & Brand Guide

## Summary
Group Scholar's marketing should make our outcomes clear, invite trust, and help schools and partners understand how we measure impact. This guide defines our positioning, voice, and approval flow so messaging stays consistent across channels.

## Positioning snapshot

- **Who we serve:** Students, educators, and program partners seeking measurable career outcomes.
- **What we deliver:** Cohort-based programs with employer engagement and coaching that convert into real placements.
- **Proof points:** Outcomes data, partner case studies, and testimonials grounded in verified metrics.

## Messaging pillars

- **Outcome-first:** Lead with measurable impact and placement results.
- **Human support:** Highlight coaching, mentorship, and community.
- **Employer-connected:** Show the employer network and hiring partnerships.
- **Equity-focused:** Emphasize access, affordability, and inclusive pathways.

## Voice & tone

- **Clear, confident, plainspoken.** Avoid buzzwords and excessive acronyms.
- **Warm and optimistic.** Center student success and partner collaboration.
- **Specific and evidence-based.** Use numbers, named partners, and examples.

## Visual direction

- **Color usage:** High-contrast and accessible. Avoid low-contrast pairs.
- **Photography:** Real students and classrooms over stock imagery.
- **Illustrations:** Use sparingly to simplify complex concepts.

## Content standards

- **Structure:** Short paragraphs, bullets, and callouts for outcomes.
- **Claims:** Every metric needs a source and date range.
- **Accessibility:** Alt text for images, descriptive headings, avoid all-caps.

## Channel guidance

- **Website:** Lead with outcomes, then program model, then partner proof.
- **Email:** One clear CTA, short subject lines, include a measurable benefit.
- **Social:** 1-2 key takeaways, include quotes or data points.
- **Press:** Route requests to comms lead, provide approved boilerplate.

## Approval flow

1. Draft content and tag the content owner.
2. Verify metrics and cite the source of truth.
3. Confirm brand voice alignment and legal review if needed.
4. Publish and log the asset in the marketing tracker.

## Core assets

- One-page overview deck
- Outcomes data snapshot
- Partner testimonial library
- Program FAQs
