# Talent & Hiring Guide

## Summary

This guide explains how Group Scholar plans roles, runs interviews, and closes hires
with clarity, speed, and a consistent candidate experience.

## Hiring philosophy

- Hire for mission alignment, craft excellence, and growth mindset.
- Use structured signals to reduce bias and keep decisions consistent.
- Move with urgency while protecting candidate dignity and time.

## Role planning checklist

- Define the outcome the role owns for the next 12 months.
- Write 3-5 must-have responsibilities and 2-3 growth areas.
- Identify the top 3 signals that prove success in the first 90 days.
- Set a compensation band and leveling expectation before sourcing.

## Interview loop blueprint

1. Recruiter screen (30 min): mission alignment, core experience, availability.
2. Hiring manager interview (45 min): scope ownership and role fit.
3. Skills or work sample (60-90 min): role-specific practical exercise.
4. Cross-functional partner (45 min): collaboration and communication.
5. Values interview (45 min): culture add and operating principles.

## Scorecard rubric

Rate each signal on a 1-4 scale with written evidence.

- Craft: execution quality, judgment, and technical/functional depth.
- Ownership: ability to lead outcomes and remove blockers.
- Collaboration: clarity, empathy, and cross-functional alignment.
- Values: alignment with mission and operating principles.

## Candidate experience standards

- Share the full interview loop in the first call.
- Provide a timeline update at least once per week.
- Give practical prep guidance for any work sample.
- Close feedback within 5 business days of the last interview.

## Offer and close

- Align on compensation, start date, and reporting line before extending.
- Share the role narrative and 90-day outcomes during the offer call.
- Confirm equipment and onboarding readiness once accepted.

## Onboarding handoff

- Provide the onboarding buddy with the role success signals.
- Schedule a 30/60/90 plan review during week two.
- Document the first-week priorities in the team workspace.
