# Mentor Program Guide

## Summary

This guide defines how Group Scholar recruits, onboards, matches, and supports mentors across scholarship programs. It standardizes expectations, safeguards, and feedback loops so mentorship remains consistent, safe, and high-impact.

## Goals

- Expand access to trusted mentors for scholars across cohorts.
- Maintain a reliable mentor pipeline with clear expectations and training.
- Protect scholars with safety protocols and escalation paths.
- Track outcomes to continuously improve mentor matching and experience.

## Mentor Types

- Career mentors: Industry guidance, resume feedback, interview prep.
- Academic mentors: Study habits, course planning, research support.
- Life skills mentors: Financial literacy, time management, confidence building.
- Alumni mentors: Near-peer guidance and lived experience.

## Recruitment & Intake

- Sourcing channels: alumni network, corporate partners, community referrals, staff outreach.
- Minimum requirements: background screen completion, availability agreement, values alignment.
- Intake form: availability, expertise, preferred mentee profiles, languages, pronouns.
- Vetting steps: reference check for new mentors, conflict-of-interest review.

## Onboarding & Training

- Orientation briefing: mission, boundaries, safeguarding, and escalation steps.
- Mentor toolkit: session templates, goal-setting prompts, sample agendas.
- Required training: youth safety, trauma-informed support, inclusive language.
- Shadowing: first session paired with a seasoned mentor when possible.

## Matching Process

- Inputs: scholar goals, schedule, preferred mentor attributes, risk flags.
- Matching priority: high-need scholars and renewal cohorts.
- Matching checklist: expertise fit, shared availability, language match, no conflicts.
- Match confirmation: both sides confirm within 5 business days.

- Standard cadence: 2 sessions per month, 45â€“60 minutes each.
- Timebound milestones: 90-day goals with quarterly reviews.
- Communication norms: email and calendar invites only; no private social channels.

## Safeguarding & Boundaries

- No 1:1 off-platform messaging; all comms logged in approved systems.
- Mandatory reporting: follow safeguarding response guide within 24 hours.
- Escalation: notify program lead and safeguarding lead immediately for concerns.
- Consent: scholars opt-in to mentorship and can pause or exit at any time.

## Support & Enablement

- Mentor office hours: monthly Q&amp;A with program lead.
- Resource library: curated toolkits, scholarship timelines, and success stories.
- Recognition: quarterly shout-outs, mentor spotlight, annual appreciation note.

## Quality & Feedback

- Session pulse survey after the first 2 sessions and quarterly thereafter.
- Coach check-ins for mentors with low feedback scores.
- Match health indicators: session attendance, scholar goal progress, sentiment.

## Reporting

- Monthly mentor roster, active match count, and engagement rate.
- Quarterly outcomes: scholar retention, goal completion, and satisfaction.
- Annual impact story bank for fundraising and reporting.

## Ownership & Cadence

- Owner: Community Engagement Lead.
- Review cadence: update playbook twice per year.
- Dependencies: safeguarding response, community engagement, alumni network.
