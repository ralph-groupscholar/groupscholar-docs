# Records & Data Lifecycle Guide

## Summary

This guide defines how Group Scholar creates, classifies, retains, archives, and disposes of records and data so that we meet legal obligations, protect scholars, and keep systems lean.

## Objectives

- Ensure consistent retention, deletion, and legal-hold practices.
- Reduce risk by minimizing unnecessary data exposure.
- Keep data discoverable, auditable, and easy to retrieve.

## Ownership & Roles

- Executive sponsor: COO
- Primary owner: Compliance + Legal
- Data stewards: Operations, Product, People Ops, Finance
- System owners: Engineering + IT

## Data Classification

- Public: Approved for external sharing.
- Internal: Day-to-day operational data.
- Confidential: Scholar, partner, or donor data.
- Restricted: Sensitive personal or financial data (PII, payment details).

## Record Lifecycle

1. Create: Capture with required metadata (owner, system, classification, purpose).
2. Use: Access is role-based and logged for restricted data.
3. Retain: Keep only the minimum required timeframe.
4. Archive: Move inactive records to lower-cost storage.
5. Dispose: Delete securely and document completion.

## Retention Standards

- Scholar application records: 7 years after decision date.
- Scholarship payment records: 10 years after final disbursement.
- Program participation logs: 5 years after completion.
- Partner contracts: 7 years after expiration.
- Donor agreements: 10 years after last gift.
- Incident and safeguarding reports: 10 years after closure.
- Support tickets and case notes: 5 years after closure.
- Marketing consent records: 5 years after opt-out.

## Legal Holds

- Legal or compliance may issue a hold that overrides deletion schedules.
- Holds must be logged with scope, start date, and release date.
- System owners confirm systems in scope and freeze deletion jobs.

## Deletion & Disposal

- Use secure deletion and confirm completion in the audit log.
- For third-party systems, request deletion certificates when available.
- Quarterly deletion reports are reviewed by Compliance.

## Archiving Guidance

- Archive by cohort or program cycle to keep retrieval simple.
- Store read-only exports with checksum validation.
- Keep an index of archived locations and owners.

## Audit & Reporting

- Monthly report: upcoming retention deadlines and holds.
- Quarterly report: deletion completion, exceptions, and risks.
- Annual review: update retention rules and classifications.

## Operating Cadence

- Monthly: data stewards review upcoming retention tasks.
- Quarterly: compliance reviews deletion logs and exceptions.
- Annually: update retention policy with legal counsel.

## Tools & Templates

- Records register (owner, system, classification, retention, archive path).
- Legal hold notice template.
- Deletion verification checklist.

## Success Metrics

- 100% of systems mapped in records register.
- <2% overdue deletions per quarter.
- Legal holds acknowledged within 5 business days.
